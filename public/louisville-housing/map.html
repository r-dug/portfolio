Methodology & assumptions

Values. Zillow Home Value Index (ZHVI) per neighborhood/ZIP. Projections: 1-yr — pooled XGBoost (momentum, valuation, market internals, macro/local features); 3-yr — mean of XGBoost and long-run trend; 5-yr and 10-yr — each area's own long-run trend (expanding-mean growth). Long horizons are trend extrapolations and carry wide uncertainty; models were selected by rolling-origin backtests 2008–2025 against naive and classical baselines.

Rents. Zillow Observed Rent Index exists only at ZIP level from ~2016. Neighborhood rents are area-weighted from containing ZIPs — never native observations. Rent projections: 1/3-yr — pooled XGBoost on rent-to-value ratios (ACS income and HUD SAFMR anchors); 5/10-yr — current rent-to-value ratio held constant. Some areas lack rent data entirely (gray).

Investment math. NOI uses an area-varying operating-expense ratio: 8% management + 6–12% maintenance (scaling with the area's housing-stock age from Census data) + the area's rental vacancy rate (Census B25004, vacant-for-rent over the rental universe; clipped 4–15%) + property tax and insurance (1.35% of value, expressed as a share of rent — a larger bite in low-yield areas). Ratios range ~39–65% across areas (median ~47%) and are shown in each tooltip. Cap rate = NOI / projected value. GRM = projected value / gross annual rent (lower is better). Total return (unlevered) = appreciation %/yr + cap rate — what a cash buyer would earn annually from operations plus price growth, before taxes and transaction costs; its range sums the component bounds (conservative). Investment score = weighted percentile blend within each horizon; the weights are yours to set in the panel (default 40% growth / 40% cap rate / 20% gross rent yield — a screening default, not a recommendation).

Boundary approximations. Ten pre-merger CDPs use 2000-vintage Census boundaries; Highlands is mapped to LOJIC "Original Highlands"; Wyandotte includes Oakdale; Eastwood is a 1.5 km buffer at its locality point; Fisherville is approximated by ZCTA 40023.

Confidence. Two kinds are shown. The panel box is the engine-wide empirical error: the production models replayed at 35 historical dates (2008–2025), errors measured against what happened. Tooltips carry per-area 80% ranges [q10 .. q90] from quantile models — the appreciation range from quantile gradient boosting on the value panel (backtest-calibrated), and the rent / cap-rate / GRM ranges from a quantile model of each ZIP's rent-to-value ratio (1/3/5-yr). Ten-year rent-derived layers carry no backtested band: they assume today's rent-to-value ratio holds, an assumption with no long-horizon evidence either way. Bands widen sharply with horizon — at 10 years projections indicate direction and relative ranking, not precise magnitudes.

Cross-check. Zillow's own metro model forecasts −0.1% for the next 12 months vs a +1.8% median area projection here — modest divergence, noted for transparency. Sources: Zillow Research, FRED, Census/ACS, HUD, LOJIC, Redfin. Built 2026-05-31.
